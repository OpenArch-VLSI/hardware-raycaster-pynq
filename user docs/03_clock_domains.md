# Clock Domains

## Distinct Clocks

The system operates across three distinct clock domains:
1. **`sysclk` (125 MHz)**: Provided by the physical PYNQ-Z2 board oscillator (Ethernet PHY). This clock is only used to drive the Xilinx PLL (`MMCME2_ADV`). No application logic runs on this clock.
2. **`serial_clk` (250 MHz)**: Synthesized by the PLL. Drives the high-speed data paths in the TMDS serialization blocks.
3. **`pixel_clk` (25 MHz)**: Synthesized by the PLL (derived as `serial_clk` / 10). Drives the core rendering engine, control logic, FSMs, and the slow-side data paths of the TMDS encoding and sync generation.

*Note: In `pynq_z2_top.sv`, `serial_clk` and `pixel_clk` are properly buffered through `BUFG` primitives.*

## Domain Allocation

### `serial_clk` Domain
- `serializer.sv`: Only the high-speed logic within the `OSERDESE2` primitive uses this clock (port `CLK`). The primitive also takes `pixel_clk` (port `CLKDIV`) to synchronize across the internal FIFO boundary.

### `pixel_clk` Domain
Almost the entire design runs on `pixel_clk`:
- `raycast_top.sv`: Control logic, coordinate assignments, memory instantiations (`map.mem`).
- `render.sv` and its submodules (`column_calc.sv`, `newton_inv.sv`, `dda.sv`): All pipeline stages, state machines, and dual-port memory buffers (`frame_buffer`, `textures`, `recode_lut`).
- `controls.sv` and its submodules (`position.sv`, `rotation.sv`): Position calculation FSMs and rotation matrix multiplications.
- `dvi_top.sv` and its submodules (`dvi_sync.sv`, `delay.sv`, `tmds_encoder.sv`): Sync counters, latency registers, and TMDS DC-balancing FSMs.

## Domain Crossings and Synchronization

### `pixel_clk` -> `serial_clk`
- **Crossing**: The 10-bit TMDS encoded symbols (`blue_tmds`, `green_tmds`, `red_tmds` in `dvi_top.sv:117,127,137`) must transition from the `pixel_clk` domain (where they are generated by `tmds_encoder`) to the `serial_clk` domain (where they are serialized by `serializer`).
- **Handling**: Handled internally by the Xilinx `OSERDESE2` primitive in `serializer.sv:37,61`. The `OSERDESE2` accepts both `CLK` (`serial_clk`) and `CLKDIV` (`pixel_clk`) and uses hard IP to safely capture the parallel data and serialize it. This is a fully synchronized, vendor-supported crossing.

### `sysclk` -> `pixel_clk`
- **Crossing**: The `pll_locked` signal from the `MMCME2_ADV` is used in `pynq_z2_top.sv:88` to hold the system in reset (`rst`). `pll_locked` is generated asynchronously relative to the output clocks.
- **Handling / Risk**: In `pynq_z2_top.sv:91`, `pll_locked` is sampled by a flip-flop driven by `pixel_clk` to increment a counter (`power_on_rst_cnt`).
- **Risky Crossing Flag**: 🚩 **Unhandled async crossing**. `pll_locked` is not explicitly passed through a 2-stage synchronizer before being evaluated in the `power_on_rst_cnt` countdown (`if (pll_locked && ...) ...`). If `pll_locked` transitions close to the active edge of `pixel_clk`, it could cause metastability in the reset counter. While often mitigated by the slow transition of a physical lock signal, standard practice requires a 2-stage synchronizer for lock-driven resets.

### Inputs -> `pixel_clk`
- **Crossing**: User button inputs (`btn_i`, `sw_i`) are driven by external mechanical switches on the board asynchronously.
- **Handling / Risk**: 🚩 **Unhandled async crossing**. In `pynq_z2_top.sv:21`, `sw_i` and `btn_i` are mapped directly to `keys`, which conceptually feed into `raycast_top` -> `controls`. There are no synchronizers (or debounce logic) on these mechanical inputs before they are evaluated in `controls.sv` and `position.sv:106`. This risks metastability in the FSMs processing the keys, though in human-scale button pushes, it may rarely manifest as a critical failure.
